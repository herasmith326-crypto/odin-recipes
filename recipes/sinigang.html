SINIGANG

Sinigang is a classic Filipino soup known for its tangy and savory flavor, usually made with pork, beef, shrimp, or fish, and cooked with tamarind or other souring agents along with vegetables like kangkong, radish, and eggplant. It’s comforting, light, and perfect with steamed rice..

INGRIDIENTS

  • Baboy (or hipon/isda)
  • Kangkong
  • Sitaw
  • Garlic
  • onnion
  • Black peppercorns
  • Water
  • Cooking oil
  • Talong
  • Sampalok (or sinigang mix)

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Boil the meat Place the pork in a pot with water and bring it to a boil until the meat becomes tender. Remove any scum that rises to the surface.
  2. Add the onion and tomatoes, then let it simmer for a few minutes to release their flavors.r.
  3. Add the radish and string beans. Cook for about 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add the eggplant, okra, and water spinach. Cook briefly so the vegetables do not overcook.
  5. Put in the sinigang mix or tamarind. Stir well and adjust the sourness to your taste.
  6. Season with fish sauce or salt, turn off the heat, and serve hot..
